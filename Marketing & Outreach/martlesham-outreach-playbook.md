# Martlesham Service Centre — Outreach Playbook

**Goal:** Land a 20-minute conversation. Not a sale. Just the meeting.
**Tone:** Local, plain-spoken, zero jargon, zero "AI hype." You're a helpful neighbour, not a salesman.

## THE CALL / VISIT — Conversation Framework

**Mindset:** You're there to *listen first*. Talk less than them. The one-pager does the selling — you just guide them through it.

### Opening (30 seconds)
> "Thanks for the time — I'll keep this short. I'm not here to sell you software. I build digital tools for local garages, and I think there's a couple of things I can do that'll bring you more work. Mind if I ask you two quick questions first?"

### Discovery — Ask, then shut up
1. **"How are most of your customers finding you right now? Word of mouth, Google, repeat?"**
   → Tells you their lead source and confidence in their digital presence.
2. **"On a busy day, roughly how many calls do you reckon go to voicemail or get missed?"**
   → They'll undershoot. The number is the wedge. Whatever they say — that's lost money.
3. **"If a customer wanted to book an MOT right now at 8pm tonight, how would they do it?"**
   → They can't. Silence does the work for you.

### The Pitch (60 seconds — slide the one-pager across)
> "So here's what I'm proposing. I've already built you a new website — booking system, WhatsApp, Google reviews, everything on this sheet. I'll run it live for 30 days completely free. No contract, no card. You see exactly how many extra bookings come through. After a month, if it's earning its keep, it's £149 a month to keep going. If not, you walk away — and you keep the website."

### Handle the Likely Objections

| They say... | You say... |
|---|---|
| *"We've already got a website."* | "I've seen it — and that's exactly why I built this one. Have a look. Takes 30 seconds on your phone. Tell me which one you'd rather a customer landed on." |
| *"We're too busy for this."* | "That's exactly the point — this runs without you touching it. The whole idea is it gives you back time, not takes it." |
| *"I don't trust this AI stuff."* | "Honestly, neither do most of my clients at first. That's why month one is free — you don't have to trust me, you just have to try it." |
| *"How much after the trial?"* | "£299 a month — that's our Starter Plan, fully managed. Works out to about £10 a day. One extra MOT a week and it's paid for itself three times over." |
| *"Let me think about it."* | "Of course. The only thing I'd say — there's no risk to starting. Worst case in 30 days, you've had a free website. Best case, you've got a system bringing in real bookings. Shall we just say I'll switch it on this week and we review on [date]?" |

### The Close — Always Ask For The Yes
> "So shall I go ahead and get it live this week? I just need your go-ahead and I'll have it running by Friday."

**Then stop talking.** Whoever speaks first loses.

### If It's a No
> "No problem at all. Can I leave the one-pager with you in case anything changes? And if you know any other local garages who might want a free month, send them my way."

→ Always leave with a referral ask. Always.

## Cadence Summary

| Day | Action |
|---|---|
| **0** | Email 1 sent (with one-pager attached) |
| **3** | Email 2 sent (follow-up) |
| **5–7** | Walk in to the workshop in person, ask for the owner by name |
| **After meeting** | Same-day thank-you text confirming next step |
| **+7 days post-meeting** | Final follow-up if no decision: *"Just checking in — happy to switch it on whenever you give the word."* |

**Rule:** No more than 3 follow-ups total. After that, leave them alone for 6 weeks, then re-approach with a new angle (a result from another local garage).
