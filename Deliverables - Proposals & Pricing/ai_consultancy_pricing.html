Setup Fee — Why It Exists & What It Covers

Why we charge a setup fee

The setup fee covers the real work done before your first monthly payment — and it ensures only serious clients engage. Businesses that pay even a small amount upfront are far more likely to follow through, share access, and see results.

It also covers your actual cost of time in month one, which is always the heaviest — typically 5–15 hours depending on the plan.

  • 1Website design, build and hosting setup — typically 4–6 hours of work
  • 2Calendly account creation, event types, calendar sync and testing
  • 3Google Business Profile claim, optimisation and booking button
  • 4Review automation setup in Make.com and MailerLite
  • 5Onboarding call, access collection and system testing
