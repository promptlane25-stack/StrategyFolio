// First question to answer

Freelance First — Company Later

Don't register a company yet. Operate as a sole trader (freelancer) — it's free, you can start today, and you only need to tell HMRC via a simple online form. Register a Ltd company only once you're consistently earning £1,500+/mo. The extra cost (Companies House fees, accountant, VAT threshold stress) is unnecessary weight right now.

Website: Don't build it in Week 1. Start with a free Carrd or Notion page as a "proof of work" link. Build the real website at the end of Month 1, once you've landed your first client — so you have a case study and testimonial ready to put on it.

// The roadmap
3-MONTH PHASES
01
Groundwork & First 2 Clients
Weeks 1–3 · Right Now
£0 spend
  • Register as sole trader on HMRC website — free, takes 10 mins. No company yet.
  • Create a simple offer — pick ONE service to start: e.g. "AI-powered social media content system" OR "automated lead follow-up for local businesses." One offer, one price.
  • Set up Instagram business profile — brand name, logo (use Canva free tier), bio that says exactly who you help and what result you deliver.
  • Build a free Carrd.co landing page (free plan) — your "link in bio." One page: what you do, who it's for, a Calendly booking link.
  • Make a list of 30 local Ipswich businesses — restaurants, salons, trades, estate agents, gyms, dentists. These are your first outreach targets.
  • DM / cold email 10 per day — personalised, short, outcome-focused. Offer a FREE audit of their current online presence as your foot in the door.
  • Don't wait to be "ready." Your first client will be landed through conversations, not a perfect website.
02
Land 3–5 Clients & Build Website
Weeks 4–8 · April–May
~£20–30/mo spend
  • Build your real website — use Framer (free tier or £14/mo paid) or Webflow. Keep it to 3 pages: Home, Services, Contact. Add your first case study immediately.
  • Start posting on Instagram 4x/week — mix of: results/case studies, "what AI can do for [local biz type]", behind-the-scenes, client wins. Use AI to generate content ideas and captions.
  • Set up Make.com (free tier) — automate your own client onboarding: new client → sends welcome email → creates folder → adds to tracker. Demonstrate this automation to clients as a taste of what you do.
  • Price your retainer packages — aim for £300–600/mo per client at this stage. At 4–5 clients you hit £1,500–2,000/mo.
  • Ask every client for a testimonial + referral — local word of mouth in Ipswich will be your fastest growth channel.
  • Set up a Google Business Profile — free. Ipswich searches for "AI marketing agency" or "social media agency Ipswich" will find you.
03
Systematise, Automate & Hit £2K
Weeks 9–12 · May–June
£2,000/mo target
  • Automate client deliverables — use AI (Claude API, ChatGPT, or n8n) to generate monthly content calendars, social posts, and reports automatically. You review and approve; AI does the drafting.
  • Create a "set and forget" monthly retainer system — client pays → automation kicks off their content pipeline → you spend max 2 hours/client/month on review.
  • Expand to Suffolk / Essex towns — Colchester, Bury St Edmunds, Felixstowe. Use the Ipswich case studies as proof.
  • Consider registering a Ltd company — once you're at £1,500+/mo consistently, it starts making tax sense. Total cost ~£50 + accountant.
  • Add a higher-tier offer — e.g. a one-time "AI workflow setup" project at £800–1,500, in addition to your monthly retainers. This accelerates cash.
  • Don't take on more clients than you can automate. 6–8 automated retainer clients at £300/mo = £2,400/mo with almost no manual work.

// Social media focus
INSTAGRAM STRATEGY
  • Bio formula: "Helping [Ipswich/Suffolk] businesses grow with AI automation 🤖 | Book a free audit 👇"
  • Content mix (4x/week): 2x educational reels ("3 things AI can do for your salon"), 1x client result/case study, 1x behind-the-scenes or personal.
  • Local hashtags: #Ipswich #IpswichBusiness #SuffolkBusiness #SuffolkSmallBusiness — small pools, less competition, highly targeted.
  • Outreach via DMs: Find local business accounts, watch their stories, then DM genuinely: "I noticed you don't have automated follow-ups — I help businesses like yours set these up. Want me to show you what it could look like?"
  • Don't obsess over followers. 200 engaged local followers will get you clients faster than 5,000 randoms. Focus on conversations, not vanity metrics.
